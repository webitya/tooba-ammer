The hour
before sleep.

Sona gives the last hour of the day a shape — a nightly sequence that leads you down into sleep, rather than a remedy taken once you are already there.

01  02  03

A sequence,
not a score.

Each evening, a scent composition begins the ritual. The Dock diffuses it gradually while the app lowers the light and softens the sound. You arrive at sleep rather than chase it.

      INDEX — COMPOSITIONS

      Compositions.

      Seven scent compositions in the series. A cycle of 28 nights is drawn from these, weighted to what you set up for.

      NO. 01

      Long Night.

      The first in the series, and the plainest. Long Night is for the evenings that will not close on their own — when the hour is late and the mind is still upright. It lengthens the descent, so that sleep is met rather than waited for.

      The composition
      • Cedarbase
      • Irisheart
      • Cool air, vetivertop
      • Diffusionslow, 30 min

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti, sleep physiologist, with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.

      NO. 02

      Still Mind.

      For the nights when the body is tired but the mind keeps working — rehearsing tomorrow, re-reading today. Still Mind is the quietest composition in the series. It loosens the grip of attention, so the thinking slows of its own accord.

      The composition
      • Mossbase
      • Bergamotheart
      • Paper, dry stonetop
      • Diffusionlow, even

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.

      NO. 03

      Across
      Time Zones.

      For the traveller whose body has landed before it has arrived. Across Time Zones works against the small disagreement between the clock on the wall and the clock within — a clear signal of which hour to believe.

      The composition
      • Vetiverbase
      • Salt, citrusheart
      • Cold mineral airtop
      • Diffusionbright, then low

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.

      NO. 04

      After a
      Hard Week.

      For the Friday evening that has to undo five days at once. After a Hard Week is the fullest composition in the series — built for the accumulated kind of tiredness, the kind that is in the shoulders as much as the eyes.

      The composition
      • Sandalwoodbase
      • Figheart
      • Rain, green airtop
      • Diffusionfull, warm

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.

      NO. 05

      The Early
      Start.

      For the night that has to end before the body wants it to — an early flight, an early room, a morning that begins in the dark. The Early Start compresses the evening cleanly, without weight, so sleep arrives ahead of its usual hour.

      The composition
      • White teabase
      • Cool mintheart
      • Linen, clean airtop
      • Diffusionbrisk, then still

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.

      NO. 06

      A Restless
      Mind.

      For the body that turns over and will not settle — when the tiredness is real but the nerves have not caught up with it. A Restless Mind is the softest composition in the series, built to quiet the body before the thoughts are even addressed.

      The composition
      • Warm amberbase
      • Lavenderheart
      • Chamomile, soft woodtop
      • Diffusionlow, enveloping

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.

      NO. 07

      Slow
      Return.

      For evenings when the day does not leave all at once — when work has ended but its hold has not. Slow Return is the most gradual composition in the series. It does not close the day quickly; it lets it recede, one quiet degree at a time.

      The composition
      • Oak, soft leatherbase
      • Black teaheart
      • Dusk air, dry herbtop
      • Diffusionunhurried, tapering

      Composed by Dr. Eve Marchetti with the Sona studio. Edinburgh, 2026.
